Chapter Chair Role

Chairs are appointed in November for a one-year term beginning in July.

Chapter Chair Roadmap

Pre-term · November

Explore the role

  • Chapters appoint chairs for the one-year journey beginning in July.
January – March

Prepare & connect

  • Review the role description and watch the chapter chair essentials video.
  • Review chapter chair resources and assess data.
  • Connect with outgoing and incoming chairs to begin the transition.
  • Build a chapter conduct committee.
  • Attend the Chapter Chair Workshop at the Global Leadership Conference.
April – June

Set the vision

  • Define your vision, objectives and goals for the year.
  • Conduct the chapter health survey and review available data.
  • If applicable, attend the Q4 regional governance meeting (e.g. RBM) and review pre-reads.
  • Host a strategic planning / game-plan session and secure approval.
July – September

Term begins

  • Schedule 1:1 onboarding with your regional chair.
  • Review chapter commitments and how chapter health is measured.
  • Host chapter ExCo / board meetings; support membership renewals.
  • Allocate financial support to your officers.
October – December

Build momentum

  • Attend the Q2 regional governance meeting and review pre-reads.
  • Get the chapter succession plan in place by 1 November.
  • Promote Officer Essentials and GLC attendance to incoming officers.
January – March (year two)

Transition knowledge

  • Review chapter health metrics and plan improvements.
  • Begin transitioning knowledge to your successor.
  • Encourage your chapter manager to attend the Chapter Manager Workshop.
April – May

Recognise & prepare

  • Prepare for the YPO Global Awards Program.
  • Support your incoming chair with a strategic planning session.
June

Hand over well

  • Ensure a proper handover to the incoming chair.
  • Send farewell notes, host a graduation ceremony and celebrate your peers.

Conduct Committee Toolkit

If it can be solved at the chapter level, solve it.
Best practices

Four moves every chapter should have in place before an issue ever arises.

01 · Set up

Establish a CCC annually

  • Confirm the committee each year; update chapter health data quarterly.
  • Recommended five members: current chair, past chair, membership / engagement officer, plus two senior board roles.
  • Avoid two members from the same forum.
02 · Process

Follow good process

  • Use member-led inquiry to gather the relevant facts.
  • Give the member under review a fair chance to respond.
  • Lean on YPO's review process, sanction guidelines and process diagram.
03 · Escalate

Communicate with the GCC

  • Ask questions early — the Global Conduct Committee is a resource.
  • Submit a Fact Finding Form or Decision Notice when action is taken.
  • This lets YPO track outcomes and support chapter decisions.
04 · Protect

Mitigate risk

  • Refine chapter bylaws with conduct, insurance and indemnification provisions.
  • Implement chapter-level waivers.
  • Obtain chapter / RALE D&O coverage.

The two-step review process

The engagement channel depends on the context of the issue. Step 1 is the fact-intensive initial review; Step 2 is the appeal, focused on due process and fairness.

Succession Planning

Chapter exco succession planning is vital for a healthy chapter:

  • Finding motivated members for each officer role strengthens chapter leadership.
  • Early officer identification allows for effective onboarding and hand-over processes that ensure the continuity of chapter initiatives and programming.
  • Timely officer identification allows officers to complete relevant role training.
  • Member engagement increases for members involved in chapter officer roles — so it's important to keep bringing new people into the succession ladder.

Succession Planning Worksheet

Discussion for a chapter executive meeting:

01 · Analyze

Current chapter process

  • What kind of succession plan does your chapter have in place now?
  • What is the status of your chapter's current succession champion pipeline?
  • How many years out have you identified champion talent to rise up the ladder?
02 · Plan

Strategize

  • How will you identify and recruit champions?
  • How many champions do you need to fill your pipeline for the next 1–3 years?
  • What strategies will you use?
  • What are ways you can recruit to increase diversity as needed?
03 · Develop

Officer training & support

  • How will you develop champions to help them successfully climb the succession ladder?
  • What programs or support will you provide?
04 · Recognise

Gratitude & recognition

  • How will you recognise champions?
  • For each position they complete, how will you acknowledge champions and ensure they continue on their champion journey?
05 · Act

Implementation

  • How will you implement your plan, and what support do you need?

Chapter Commitments Defined

New New metric this year Revised Existing metric with revisions

  1. Chapter Charter New

    Chapter chair signed or acknowledged the YPO chapter charter (formerly the TLA) during the current fiscal year.

  2. Chapter Bylaws

    Bylaws (or equivalent legal documentation in the country of incorporation) updated within the current or previous three fiscal years.

  3. Chapter Strategic Plan

    A chapter strategic plan created or updated within the current or previous three fiscal years.

  4. Core Officer Elect Identification Revised

    All elect core officer roles identified before 1 November (CC, ALO, LO, FO, MO for YPO/Integrated; CC, ALO, LO, FO, MEO for YPO Gold).

  5. Current Core Officers Trained Revised

    Four or more current core officers completed officer training before 1 July.

  6. Chapter RBM Attendance

    The chapter chair or an officer proxy attended a Regional Board Meeting during the past fiscal year.

  7. Chapter Conduct Committee New

    Three or more chapter conduct committee (CCC) members identified for the current fiscal year.

  8. Chapter Manager Workshop

    Current chapter manager attended a Chapter Manager Workshop within the current or past three fiscal years.

  9. Learning Calendar Revised

    A minimum of four events uploaded by 1 October via Cvent or the Chapter Management Hub.

  10. Trained Forum Moderators

    Every active member forum has a current, trained forum moderator.

  11. Forum Training Revised

    All primary members who joined on or after 1 July 2024 were forum trained within their first six months.

  12. Forum Data New

    Chapter forum data reviewed and updated on the Chapter Management Hub during the current fiscal year.

  13. Chapter Membership New

    The chapter roster is verified as accurate and all listed members have paid chapter and YPO global dues for the year.

  14. Minimum Membership Requirements Revised

    Chapter meets minimum membership: approved before 1 July 2024 = 16 primary members; after = 21 primary members.
